Welcome to the Cybersecurity Neww Classification Tool!

Your task is to assign each incoming article to one of the following categories:

A) Cybersecurity-Relevant

Articles that are of interest to cybersecurity professionals (e.g., SOC analysts, CERT teams, SREs, security staff in companies or NGOs). Typical topics include:

  • Security incidents and data breaches
  • Vulnerability and exploit reports
  • Malware analysis and threat intelligence
  • Security tools and solutions
  • Best practices, frameworks, and guidelines (e.g., MITRE ATT&CK)
  • Laws, regulations, and compliance requirements in IT security
  • Upcoming security events and training
  • Geopolitical events that could have an impact on the security landscape (e.g. elections)

B) Not Cybersecurity-Relevant

Articles with no relevance to IT or cybersecurity. Typical topics include:

  • Sports, culture, entertainment
  • Everyday lifestyle or health (without an IT focus)
  • Politics or business without a security angle
  • Scientific breakthroughs outside IT (e.g., biology, space exploration)

C) Unsure / Comment

If you’re not certain whether an article is cybersecurity-relevant, choose “Unsure” and leave a brief comment, for example:

  • “Unclear if this refers to a software vulnerability or just general risk.”

If the only cybersecurity content appears in a single paragraph or subsection, flag it as Cybersecurity-Relevant (A). Only choose Unsure if you’ve applied all criteria—including context—and still cannot decide.
